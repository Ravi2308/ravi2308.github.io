Ravi Shankar Mishra

Hi! I’m Ravi Shankar Mishra, a Machine Learning Engineer and Researcher specializing in Computer Vision, Generative AI, and Video Understanding.

I completed my MS (by Research) from IIIT Hyderabad, where I was part of the CVIT lab under the guidance of Prof. C.V. Jawahar, Dr. Ravi Kiran S., and Dr. Anbumani Subramanian. My thesis focused on predictive modeling of accident-prone zones and action recognition in unstructured traffic scenarios using ADAS data at scale.

My research includes projects such as IDD-CRS and Transfer-LMR. I also contributed to Project iRASTE, a large-scale road safety initiative that received an Honourable Mention at the ACM India OAIC Award 2026.

Prior to this, I worked as an Applied Research Engineer at IHub-Data and as a Research Intern at Bennett University under the guidance of Dr. Deepak Garg and Dr. Gaurav Singal.

Currently, I work in the Generative AI domain, building scalable systems for image generation, retrieval, and efficient inference, with a focus on bridging research and real-world deployment in multimodal AI systems.

Research

I am passionate about advancing vision-language tasks, multimodal systems, embodied AI, and diffusion models. My goal is to develop intelligent systems with multimodal sensing, spatio-temporal reasoning, and natural language generation capabilities.

IDD-CRS: A Comprehensive Video Dataset for Critical Road Scenarios in Unstructured Environments
Ravi Shankar Mishra, Anbumani Subramanian, CV Jawahar, Ravi Kiran Sarvadevabhatla
Under Review EEE Intelligent Vehicles(IV), 2025

We present a large video dataset of critical road scenarios, with precise annotations obtained using an Advanced Driving Assistance Systems (ADAS), and benchmarked for video action recognition and long-tail action recognition tasks. This dataset is the first to focus on the behaviors of pedestrians, other vehicles, and the ego car, making it ideal for road safety research.

Transfer-LMR: Heavy-Tail Driving Behavior Recognition in Diverse Traffic Scenarios
Chirag Parikh, Ravi Shankar Mishra, Rohan Chandra, Ravi Kiran Sarvadevabhatla,
arXiv Under Review EEE Intelligent Vehicles(IV), 2025

We present Transfer-LMR, a modular training routine that improves the recognition of driving behaviors, including rare ones often overlooked by existing methods. Tested on METEOR and HDD datasets, our approach shows significant performance gains across all behavior classes..
Enhancing Road Safety: Predictive Modeling of Accident-Prone Zones with ADAS-Equipped Vehicle Fleet Data
Ravi Shankar Mishra, Dev Singh Thakur, Anbumani Subramanian, Mukti Advani , S Velmurugan, Juby Jose , CV Jawahar, Ravi Kiran Sarvadevabhatla
IEEE Intelligent Vehicles Symposium (IV), 2024

A novel approach to identifying possible early accident-prone zones in a large city-scale road network using geo-tagged collision alert data from a vehicle fleet
Corridor segmentation for automatic robot navigation in indoor environment using edge devices
Surbhi Gupta, R Sangeeta, Ravi Shankar Mishra, Gaurav Singal, Tapas Badal, Deepak Garg
Computer Networks Journal, 178, 2020

Developed a high-precision, real-time corridor segmentation system for indoor robot navigation using efficient edge-based vision perception and sensors.
